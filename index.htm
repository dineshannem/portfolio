Underwater Image Enhancement

Enhanced underwater photos using Fourier- and spatial-domain methods, plus CNN-based post-processing to restore color and detail.

Implemented a multi-stage pipeline targeting the common problems of underwater imagery: color cast, reduced contrast, blurring caused by scattering, and loss of fine detail. The first stage performs white balance and color normalization using histogram-based methods to reduce dominant blue/green hues.

Next, a frequency-domain upsampling routine preserves high-frequency edges while increasing apparent resolution. Spatial domain filters (adaptive sharpening and dehazing priors) reduce haze and enhance edges without introducing ringing artifacts.

A lightweight CNN was trained on paired degraded/clean examples to refine contrast and texture. Training used data augmentation (brightness, blur, and noise) to increase robustness to real-world underwater conditions.

I evaluated results with objective metrics (PSNR, SSIM) and perceptual checks. For deployment, the pipeline is optimized for CPU inference and includes options to balance speed vs. fidelity.

Tools & libraries: Python, OpenCV, NumPy, TensorFlow/Keras, and custom Fourier routines. Results show visible restoration of color and improved detail in coral and underwater scenes.

Soil Irrigation System using 8051

A microcontroller-based irrigation controller that automates watering based on real-time soil-moisture measurements.

Designed and implemented an embedded controller using the 8051 microcontroller family to manage irrigation pumps and valves based on analog soil moisture sensor inputs and configurable thresholds.

The system performs on-device calibration for sensor variability and uses hysteresis to avoid frequent on/off cycling of pumps, increasing hardware longevity and saving water.

A simple UART interface and an LCD status display provide local monitoring; for advanced versions the design includes an optional wireless module to send telemetry to a PC or mobile app.

Power-management features (sleep modes and low-power sampling) reduce energy consumption for battery-backed or solar-powered installations. Safety features include pump-failure detection and manual override input.

Tools used: 8051 assembly and Embedded C for firmware, Proteus for simulation, and prototyping with breadboarded sensors and relays. Achieved reliable automated watering cycles and reduced water use in test plots.

Concrete pipes

An overview study of concrete pipes, their durability, and their importance in modern infrastructure projects.

Conducted a detailed study on the use of Concrete Spun Pipes (CSP) in drainage systems, culverts, irrigation, and urban infrastructure. Highlighted their long service life compared to alternative materials due to their natural resistance to corrosion, abrasion, and environmental damage. Reviewed manufacturing processes including centrifugal spinning, curing, and reinforcement that improve structural strength. Analyzed how reinforced concrete pipes withstand heavy soil loads and traffic pressure in underground installations. Studied the role of CSP in stormwater management, ensuring proper drainage during heavy rains and reducing flooding risks. Compared CSP with PVC and steel alternatives, noting advantages in sustainability, cost-effectiveness, and load-bearing capacity. Identified challenges such as transportation weight, installation effort, and maintenance requirements in large-scale projects. Investigated how CSP standards (e.g., IS:458 for India) ensure quality, uniformity, and reliability across different projects. Emphasized their environmental contribution, as concrete is locally available, recyclable, and has lower long-term lifecycle costs. Concluded that concrete pipes remain a preferred choice for critical infrastructure due to their strength, durability, and adaptability.
